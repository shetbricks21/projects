An American omelette (or "omelet"as they are sometimes spelled), starts out in the same way as a "french omelette", but as the eggs cook, the edges are lifted from the sides of the pan with a spatula so the runny eggs can flow underneath.

When the eggs are nearly set, the filling is added and the omelette is folded in half rather than rolled.
